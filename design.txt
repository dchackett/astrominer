ASTROMINER

A programming game based on an RTS-like iteration of the classic arcade game Asteroids. There are two teams, Red and Blue. Their objective: destroy the other team's space station! Players play by writing the AI that controls all of their units.

The game mechanics are based around Asteroids-style Newtonian mechanics. We want to enforce a coherent set of rules to allow for emergent gameplay. All objects have masses, velocities, etc. Motion is achieved by applying thrust/force!

We have a preliminary implementation of an RTS-style version of the gameplay logic, but we'll want to rebuild around the idea of making this a programming game. Here are the requirements:
- First-class support for graphical execution to watch gameplay, but maybe we want support for headless execution too
- Ability to drop in code for the unit/army AI so players can iterate easily
- Factorization between core game logic (immutable by players) and unit/army AI
- Whatever logging and telemetry are necessary for coding agents to test different approaches; this can be prints they write into their AI code, but also we probably want some agent-friendly way of watching games / inspecting game states / etc.
- Some way of running experiments/scenarios short of full gameplay?

RELEVANT CONCEPTS:

UNITS: stations, rockets, tugs
OTHER OBJECTS: bullets, asteroids
ALSO: tractor beams, repair beams

GAMEPLAY
- No fog of war
- Red team and blue team (unit wireframe colors)
- Toroidal boundary conditions

GRAPHICAL INTERFACE
- Movable camera
- No interaction with game environment, just for viewing

COLLISIONS AND DAMAGE
- All objects are wireframes. Collisions are per the wireframe.
- Bullets cause damage when they hit things
- Collisions between any pair of objects cause damage proportional to the exchange of momentum
- Soft collisions don't cause damage
- Collision avoidance is the responsibility of per-player AI code (as is all unit behavior)

ASTEROIDS
- Come in many different sizes/tiers
- When damaged sufficiently, break into many asteroids of the next smaller tier
- Smallest asteroids can be returned to the station as resources
- Gameplay starts with many asteroids seeded around the map

TRACTOR BEAMS
- Tugs and stations have tractor beams
- Tractor beams can apply force in arbitrary directions on a single object
- Limited range
- Used by tugs and stations for resource gathering
- Can be used to deflect bullets, throw things, etc
- Have some cooldown time so they can't deflect arbitrarily many bullets easily

STATION
- Home base, produces tugs and rockets
- Huge, very massive, but can be moved by tugs; otherwise immobile (no thrusters)
- Has up to 5 tractor beams
- Has a limited supply of "tractor beam force" which it can distribute amongst its tractor beams however it wants
- When smallest tier of asteroid contacts the station, the station eats it and turns it into resources
- Uses tractor beams to pull in nearby small rocks (handled by per-player AI)
- Uses tractor beams to push away big rocks, bullets, etc (handled by per-player AI)
- Has a "repair beam" of limited range that can repair friendly units for some resource cost
- Can slowly repair itself for some resource cost

BULLETS
- Small mass, explode when hitting something to cause damage rather than causing damage via collision dynamics
- Can be deflected by tractor beams
- Limited range (die after existing for a while)

ROCKETS
- The A-frame rocket from Asteroids
- Rotates
- Thruster to accelerate in the direction its facing
- Shoots bullets forwards
- Bullets are fired with relative velocity vs the rocket 
- In resource gathering, it must blow up large asteroids so that tugs can gather small asteroids (per-player AI must do this)
- Combat unit (shoots bullets)

TUGS
- Squares
- Omnidirectional thrusters
- Tractor beam to move things around
- Gathers resources by dragging small rocks back to base (per-player AI must do this)
- Lots of potential creative applications of tractor beam, e.g., point defense, throwing rocks (per-player AI)
